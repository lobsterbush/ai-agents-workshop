# Sample Transcripts: Perspectives on Political Representation

## Data Source

**Title:** Perspectives on Political Representation  
**Repository:** Qualitative Data Repository (QDR)  
**DOI:** [10.5064/F6L9HHYL](https://doi.org/10.5064/F6L9HHYL)  
**Authors:** Ruedin & Murahwa

## About the Data

This dataset contains **25 de-identified semi-structured interviews** with regional elected representatives in South Africa. The research explores whether minority representatives feel pressure to represent their associated group.

### Research Questions
- Do minority representatives feel obligated to prioritize their group's interests?
- How do representatives navigate tensions between descriptive and substantive representation?
- What role does trust play in political representation?

### Sample Details
- **N:** 25 interviews
- **Participants:** Regional elected representatives (de-identified with pseudonyms)
- **Location:** South Africa
- **Format:** PDF transcripts

### Key Themes (Example Output)

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Descriptive Representation Pressure | Expectations that minority representatives prioritize their group's interests |
| Legitimacy Negotiations | Navigating questions about qualification vs. identity-based selection |
| Constituency Boundary Tensions | Conflict between geographic vs. identity constituencies |
| Trust Building | Strategies for building trust across group boundaries |
| Strategic Essentialism | Tactical use of group identity for political purposes |
